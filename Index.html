Wire carbon content matters more than knot style. High-tensile (HT) = high-carbon steel. Both hinge joint and fixed knot fences can be HT. All products in this tracker are confirmed high-carbon / Class 3 or Zinc-Aluminum coated.

Galvanization comparison:

  • Zn/Al XtraLife (Summit Steel) — Zinc-aluminum alloy coating. Outlasts Class 3 zinc by 2–3×. Best corrosion protection available in this category.
  • Class 3 — ~3× more zinc than Class 1. Industry standard for HT fence. Good for most climates including Colorado.
  • Class 1 — Not included in this tracker. Standard farm fence, lower lifespan.

Knot type tradeoffs:

  • Fixed knot — Strongest, most rigid. Best impact resistance. Stays where you put it. Preferred for sheep/goats and predator pressure.
  • Square knot — Slightly more flexible than fixed. Still HT. Good all-purpose.
  • Hinge joint — Flexes under pressure and springs back. Traditional field fence behavior. Fine for cattle/horses with HT wire. Less ideal for small ruminants.

Stay spacing for sheep: 6" or tighter preferred to prevent head entrapment. 12" stay is adequate for Katahdin/Barbados cross if animals are not horned. Bekaert Horseman 2x4 is the most secure for small ruminants but at significant cost premium.
