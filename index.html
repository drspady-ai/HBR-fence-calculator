Carbon content & tensile strength

Wire carbon content matters more than knot style. High-tensile (HT) = high-carbon steel. Both hinge joint and fixed knot fences can be HT. All products in this tracker are confirmed high-carbon / Class 3 or Zinc-Aluminum coated.

Galvanization comparison

  • Zn/Al XtraLife (Summit Steel) — Zinc-aluminum alloy coating. Outlasts Class 3 by 2–3×. Best corrosion protection available in this category.
  • Class 3 — ~3× more zinc than Class 1. Industry standard for HT fence. Good for Colorado climates.
  • Class 1 — Not included. Standard farm fence, lower lifespan.

Knot type tradeoffs

  • Fixed knot — Strongest, most rigid. Best impact resistance. Stays where you put it. Preferred for sheep/goats and predator pressure.
  • Square knot — Slightly more flexible than fixed. Still HT. Good all-purpose.
  • Hinge joint — Flexes under pressure and springs back. Fine for cattle/horses with HT wire. Less ideal for small ruminants.

Stay spacing for sheep

6" or tighter preferred to prevent head entrapment. 12" stay is adequate for Katahdin/Barbados cross if animals are not horned. Bekaert Horseman 2×4 is the most secure for small ruminants but carries a significant cost premium.

Panels for sheep & dogs

For Katahdins and dog containment, the 4×4 Utility Panel (6 ga) is the workhorse. The 2×4 Horse Panel (4 ga) is heavier-duty and more escape-proof for dogs. Hog panels (4 ga, graduated) are excellent for smaller ruminant separation with denser lower spacing.

Panel gauge notes

  • 1 ga Bull Panel — Extremely heavy (6"×6" mesh). Overkill for sheep, designed for bull confinement.
  • 4 ga panels — Standard for cattle & hog panels. Stockyard's 2×4 Horse Panel is 4 ga, stiffer than typical 6 ga utility.
  • 6 ga utility panels — Most common utility panel. Good strength-to-cost for general livestock, dogs, and interior pens.
